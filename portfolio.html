BDO Project

Project Overview: BioCane Business Model

The project focused on establishing BioCane, a business producing bioplastic from sugarcane, aiming to contribute to sustainable development. The business was situated in the Somkhele mining community in KwaZulu-Natal, which faced economic decline due to the closure of a local mine. BioCane sought to:

  • Leverage local resources: Partnered with a local sugarcane farmer for raw materials.
  • Tackle environmental challenges: Addressed the environmental impact of traditional plastic by producing biodegradable plastics from sugarcane, reducing pollution.
  • Job creation: BioCane aimed to revitalize the local economy by creating jobs in the production facility and implementing sustainable energy solutions such as solar panels and boreholes to support both production and the community.

Download the BDO Project Case Study below.

Case Study

Project Execution

I worked in a group of six members, each managing different business functions. The project included financial planning with a 5-year budget, operations delegation, SWOT and PESTELE analyses, risk management, and compliance with sustainable development goals (SDGs).

Final Project Submission

The final submission for the BioCane project included a comprehensive set of documents that outlined every aspect of the business model. The cover letter, cover page, and contents page introduced the submission. The ownership and management section detailed the structure of the business, followed by the vision and mission statements that set BioCane’s long-term goals. The executive summary provided a concise overview of the entire business plan. In-depth sections like the market strategy, technology use, and SWOT analysis showcased the competitive advantage and operational model. The PESTELE analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal, and Ethical) and triple bottom line addressed how the business aligned with sustainable development. Shareholder analysis and positioning in the industry for growth further reinforced BioCane’s potential. The project also analyzed the SDGs addressed (Sustainable Development Goals), global and South African competitors, and included a risk analysis. Detailed sections on the target market, operations delegation, and a 5-year budget (covering costs, revenues, and profits) offered financial projections. Additionally, we incorporated insights from ChatGPT responses to refine our strategy. The project concluded with a detailed cost breakdown and supporting references. Deloitte CA of the Future Project

Project Overview: AI in Shipping for Smith’s (Pty) Ltd

In this project, you and your team developed a digital transformation strategy for Smith’s, a chocolate factory, focusing on incorporating Artificial Intelligence (AI) into the company's shipping process. The project was part of the Deloitte CA of the Future initiative, which aimed to expose students to the SAICA CA (2025) framework, emphasizing digital disruption, value creation, critical thinking, and integrated thinking.

Download the Deloitte CA(SA) of the Future Project Case Study below.

Case Study

Project Execution

  1. AI Integration in the Shipping Process:
    • I explored how AI technologies can be used to digitize Smith's existing shipping and invoicing process. This included automating order management, inventory tracking, delivery verification, and payment processing using AI-driven tools.
    • Key technologies involved:
      • AI-driven inventory management for tracking stock levels.
      • Automated invoicing systems.
      • Smart delivery tracking for real-time updates.
    • These implementations were designed to increase operational efficiency, reduce manual errors, and streamline the revenue business process.
  2. Risk Assessment:

    We conducted a thorough risk analysis on the digitization of the shipping process. The risks identified included cybersecurity threats, system failures, and the potential for AI miscommunication, impacting the triple bottom line (financial, social, and environmental factors).

  3. Employee Training for AI:

    Recognizing the risks of staff not fully understanding AI, the project emphasized the need for employee training. We proposed tailored programs to upskill employees, allowing them to effectively leverage AI in daily operations.

  4. IAS 2 and Inventory Costs:

    The project also applied IAS 2 to identify the costs of purchase and conversion involved in producing Smith's chocolate bars. You provided four clear examples of these costs and how they are accounted for under the standard.

Final Project Submission

My group and I presented the project in the form of a report and a 10-minute presentation, as required by the given case study(above). The report covered our digital strategy, risk analysis, and recommendations for employee training and inventory cost management, while the presentation used slides and visual aids to engage the marker. This project demonstrates my ability to apply digital transformation strategies to real-world business problems, showing how AI and other technologies can drive value in traditional industries.
